Statistics

Global access to drinking water has improved, but it is still a problem in many areas. Here are some statistics:

  • 785 million people lack basic drinking water services.
  • 2 billion people use a drinking water source contaminated with feces.
  • Every day, over 800 children under age 5 die from diarrhea attributable to poor water and sanitation.

Importance of a Safe Water Supply in India

  • Economic Burden

    • Waterborne diseases impose an economic burden of approximately USD 600 million annually in India, especially affecting drought- and flood-prone areas, which have impacted a third of the population recently.

  • Groundwater Depletion

    • Two-thirds of India's 718 districts face extreme water depletion. India, the world's highest user of groundwater, has rapidly depleted its supplies due to extensive drilling. Groundwater provides 85% of rural and 48% of urban drinking water needs (Source: JMP 2017).

  • Child Rights and Water Access

    • According to the Convention on the Rights of a Child, all children have the right to clean water and basic sanitation. UNICEF aims to ensure every child fulfills this right.

  • Impact on Education

    • Lack of a safe and reliable water source often forces women and children to collect water. This responsibility leads to decreased school attendance, with a 22% increase in dropout rates reported in drought-affected states.

  • Women and Water Collection

    • Approximately 54% of rural women and some adolescent girls spend an estimated 35 minutes daily collecting water, equivalent to 27 lost days' wages annually (Source: Analysis of the Situation of Children, Adolescents, and Women in India 2016).

  • Access to Safe Water

    • In 2015, India achieved 93% coverage of access to improved water supply in rural areas. However, the shift to Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) revealed that less than 49% of the rural population uses safely managed drinking water (Source: JMP 2017).
